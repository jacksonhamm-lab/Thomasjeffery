Before You Book

The decisions
that matter.

Most wedding suit questions come down to four things. Here's what to know before you walk in.

Select any to learn more.

01 Suit or Tuxedo? Most weddings, the answer is a suit.
+

A tuxedo is the right call for a very formal evening wedding, a black-tie dress code, or a venue where the setting genuinely warrants it. For the vast majority of Calgary weddings, whether daytime, outdoor, hotel ballroom, or otherwise, a well-fitted suit is the more appropriate choice and photographs better across different party members. When in doubt, come in and we'll tell you honestly.

02 Custom or Off the Rack? Your timeline decides this one.
+

If your wedding is more than four months out, custom through Coppley is a real option. Your suit is built to your exact measurements, in the fabric you choose, and ready well before the day. Under four months, ready-to-wear selected and fitted in-store is the smarter play. We do this constantly and the results are excellent. Either way, alterations are handled in-house on our schedule, so build that time into your planning.

03 Coordinating the Party Matching and coordinating are different things.
+

The groom should stand out. A slightly different suit, a different tie, or a unique pocket square is usually enough to set him apart. Groomsmen can coordinate without being identical: the same suit color in different cuts, or the same cut in a slightly different shade. Ties and pocket squares are the easiest way to pull a group together without making everyone look like a uniform. We'll help you figure this out in the fitting.

04 What Goes Under It The shirt and tie make or break the suit.
+

A white dress shirt with a spread collar is almost never wrong. Avoid pre-tied ties. It shows in photos. The pocket square can match the tie exactly for a formal, polished look, or complement it for something slightly more relaxed. Both are correct depending on the occasion. These are the details that separate a great look from a good one, and they're easy to get right when someone walks you through them.

How far out
are you?

Your date changes everything about how we approach your suit. Here's what to expect at each stage.

6+ Months Out Every option is open.
+

The ideal window for Coppley custom. Suits ship in roughly a month, leaving plenty of time for fittings, adjustments, and any unexpected delays. Come in, explore fabrics, and take your time. If you're dressing a full party, earlier is always better. Party orders need to be brought in and that adds lead time.

3–6 Months Out Custom is still on the table.
+

Custom works comfortably at this stage for most specifications. Shipping takes about a month, so you have a solid buffer for fittings and finishing. Off-the-rack party orders typically take about 2 weeks to bring in the full run of sizes. Come in soon and we'll map everything out.

6–12 Weeks Out Doable — but come in now.
+

Custom may still be possible depending on the specifications. We'll tell you honestly. Off-the-rack sizes for a party take about 2 weeks to order in if we don't have them on hand. Either way, book your appointment this week. The sooner we have a plan, the better your options.

Under 6 Weeks Call us. We'll figure it out.

Why These Brands

The suits we reach for.

Every brand we carry was chosen deliberately. For weddings, these are the three we recommend most, and the reasons why.

Paul Betenly Canada · Est. 1981

Our go-to for wedding parties. Consistent fit, excellent fabric quality, and a price point that's fair when you're dressing four or more. Hand-sewn armholes and pick stitching throughout. The current offer makes this the most value-forward way to dress your whole group.

Coppley Canada · Est. 1883

Available ready-to-wear in-store and through our custom program. Half-canvas construction means the jacket molds to your body rather than holding a generic shape. For the groom who wants something built to his exact measurements, the custom program offers four seasons of fabric, made in Canada. The suit you'll still be wearing in ten years.

Jack Victor Montreal, Canada · Est. 1913

Fabrics selected in Europe, everything cut and sewn in Montreal. A strong choice for fathers of the wedding or anyone in the party who wants a sport jacket instead of a suit. The wool quality at this price point is genuinely hard to match, and the dress pants coordinate well with the rest of the group.
